## The Bottleneck Was Never Intelligence

Smart people have always existed. Domain experts. Polymaths. The constraint was never raw capability.

The constraint was **bandwidth**.

One human can only:
- Hold so much context
- Switch between domains so fast
- Execute at the speed of thought

JARVIS removed that constraint for Stark. Real-time calculations. Parallel simulations. Instant research. No ego. No politics. No "let's schedule a meeting to align on requirements."

We have JARVIS now. We call it Claude, GPT, Grok. And Forge for the math.

## The Protocol

For those who want to replicate this:

1. **AI as architect, not autocomplete.** Use Claude/GPT for system design, not just code generation.

2. **Deterministic math.** Never trust AI for calculations. Use tools that compute, not predict.

3. **Verified sources.** AI hallucinates citations. Build verification into your workflow.

4. **Zero meetings.** Every meeting is coordination overhead. Every approval process is latency. Cut them all.

5. **Iterate in hours.** If something breaks, fix it immediately. Don't schedule a retro.

6. **Ship constantly.** 23 commits in one day is normal. Waiting for "perfect" is waste.

The Stark Protocol isn't about being a genius. It's about removing the bottlenecks that prevent execution.

AI is the removal mechanism.
